The solar system is a vast cosmic neighborhood that
includes our sun, planets, moons, asteroids, comets, and
other celestial bodies. At the center of it all is the sun, a
hot, glowing ball of gas. Orbiting around the sun are
planets, including Earth, each with its own unique
characteristics. Moons are natural satellites that orbit
planets, and asteroids and comets are rocky or icy
objects that also move through space. Gravity, a force of
attraction, keeps everything in our solar system
in motion. It's like a celestial dance orchestrated by the
sun's gravitational pull.

What are lunar rovers?
. A lunar rover or Moon rover is a space exploration vehicle designed to move across the surface of the Moon. The Apollo Program’s Lunar Roving Vehicle was driven on the Moon by members of three American crews, Apollo 15, 16, and 17. Other rovers have been partially or fully autonomous robots, such as the Soviet Union's Lunoghots, Chinese Yutus, and the Indian Pragyan. Four countries have had operating rovers on the Moon: the Soviet Union, the United States, China and India.
